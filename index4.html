The little prince

"The Little Prince" (fr. Le Petit Prince) is an allegorical fairy tale, the most famous work of Antoine de Saint-Exupery.

The tale tells of the Little Prince who visits various planets in space, including Earth. The book addresses themes of loneliness, friendship, love and loss. Despite the style of a children's book, its hero talks about life and human nature[2].

First published April 6, 1943 in New York. It has sold over 140 million copies worldwide, making it one of the best-selling books.

The drawings in the book are made by the author himself and are no less famous than the book itself. These are not only illustrations, but also an organic part of the work: the heroes of the tale themselves refer to the drawings and argue about them. The unique illustrations in The Little Prince break down language barriers and become part of a universal visual lexicon understandable to everyone.

>After all, all adults were children at first, only few of them remember this,” Antoine de Saint-Exupery, from a dedication to the book.
